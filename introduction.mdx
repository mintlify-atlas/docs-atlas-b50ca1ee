OmniVoice is a zero-shot multilingual text-to-speech model that generates high-quality speech from text alone — no fine-tuning required. It supports over 600 languages, delivers fast inference, and gives you full control over the output voice through either voice cloning or voice design.

## Architecture Overview

OmniVoice is built on a **diffusion language model** architecture — a clean, streamlined, and scalable design that combines the strengths of diffusion models (high output quality, controllable generation) with the efficiency of language model decoding.

The model is distributed via HuggingFace as `k2-fsa/OmniVoice` and loaded using the `OmniVoice.from_pretrained()` API. It outputs audio as a list of `torch.Tensor` with shape `(1, T)` at **24 kHz**.

Three generation modes are supported:

- **Voice Cloning** — provide `ref_audio` (and optionally `ref_text`) to clone a speaker's voice
- **Voice Design** — provide an `instruct` string describing the desired speaker attributes
- **Auto Voice** — omit all prompts and let the model pick a voice automatically
